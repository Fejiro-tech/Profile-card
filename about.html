ABOUT ME

Bio

Hi, my name is Priscillia Osumaka, I'm a Frontend developer who loves turning ideas into clean, intuitive web interfaces.

I've always wanted to build a meaningful career — one that challenges me, inspires me, and allows me to create real impact. Although I originally studied Political Science, I found myself drawn to technology and creativity. I wanted to do something different, something that let me combine logic with design.

That curiosity led me to explore frontend development, and I instantly fell in love with the process of bringing ideas to life through code. Today, I'm proud to be a frontend developer who's constantly learning, building, and improving.

For me, tech isn't just a career switch — it's a passion that reflects who I am: a problem-solver, a creator, and a woman determined to make her mark in the digital world.

Professional Goals

My goals during the HNG Internship are focused on growth as a frontend developer and contributing effectively to real-world projects.

  • Deepen knowledge of frontend technologies, especially React and modern JavaScript.
  • Contribute meaningfully to real-world projects while collaborating with other developers.
  • Strengthen problem-solving and critical thinking abilities through hands-on challenges.
  • Build better habits around clean code, version control, and project structure.
  • Grow professional confidence and communication skills in a team environment.
  • Prepare for future roles as a skilled and reliable frontend engineer.

Areas of Low Confidence

While I am continuously learning and improving, there are certain areas where I feel less confident and am actively working to grow.

  • Backend development: Still learning server-side technologies and databases.
  • Advanced algorithms and data structures: Working to improve problem-solving skills in this area.
  • Version control collaboration: Handling complex Git workflows and merge conflicts can be challenging.
  • Public speaking or presenting technical work: Can feel nervous explaining projects to larger audiences.
  • Testing and debugging large-scale applications: Gaining experience but handling complex bugs in big projects is still an area of growth.
